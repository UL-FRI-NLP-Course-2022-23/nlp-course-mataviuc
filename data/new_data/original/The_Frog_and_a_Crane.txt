A frog invited a crane to a meal and served it in a shallow dish. The crane could not eat and begged the frog to help him. The frog replied that he would not lift a claw to help one who had not the wits to find a way out of his difficulty for himself. So the crane set to work and improvised a long neck, and so was able to reach the food and eat. The frog then boasted of his sharp wits, but the crane replied: 'If you were as good as your word, friend, I should now be at the bottom of your well.
